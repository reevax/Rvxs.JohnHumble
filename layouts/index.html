Will Writing

Your will is one the most important documents you will ever write.

It states your wishes on death and you decide what happens to your property and finances after you die.

Writing a will enables you to:

Decide the destination of your estate on death to avoid the Intestacy Rules.

Decide on your executors and trustees.

Appoint one or more guardians to look after your infant children.

Leave gifts of specific items or fixed sums of money.

Take advantage of inheritance tax planning and asset protection.

Leave directions as to burial or other funeral arrangements.

Lasting Powers of Attorney

The LPA for property and finance covers your money and property and can be used as soon as it is registered so that in the event of mental incapacity your attorney acts for you and not The Court of Protection.

The LPA for health & welfare covers your personal healthcare if you are mentally incapable of making those decisions for yourself. As well as daily tasks, it can cover power for your attorney(s) to refuse or agree to any medical treatment you may need to stay alive if you are ever unable to make that decision.

Make your Lasting Powers of Attorney and ensure that your family, rather than the Court of Protection, looks after:

Your Property

Your Money

Your Health

Your Welfare
